Great software comes from great teams. After building multiple engineering organizations from the ground up, we've developed a framework for creating teams that are both productive and happy.

## Hiring for Impact

We hire for three things in order:

1. **Attitude** — Willingness to learn and collaborate
2. **Aptitude** — Problem-solving ability and technical curiosity
3. **Experience** — Relevant domain knowledge

## Team Structure

Small, autonomous teams consistently outperform large ones:

- **4-6 engineers per team** — Small enough to stay agile
- **Clear ownership** — Each team owns specific business domains
- **Reduced dependencies** — Teams can ship without waiting on others

## Communication Patterns

Effective communication is the foundation of high-performing teams:

- **Async-first** — Write things down, use sync for decisions
- **Daily standups** — 10 minutes, no longer
- **Weekly demos** — Share progress, get feedback early

## Continuous Improvement

The best teams are always getting better:

- **Retrospectives** — Honest reflection every iteration
- **Technical discussions** — Regular time for architecture and design
- **Learning budgets** — Time and resources for growth

Building a great engineering team takes intentional effort, but the results — in productivity, quality, and retention — make it worthwhile.
